Doing calibration the hard way

Lesson #99

Theory

All sensors require calibration. There are two easy ways to handle calibration:

Easy way #1: Do nothing. Assume that the manufacturer’s calibration is good enough for you.

Easy way #2: Use a higher quality instrument as a reference and map your sensor’s output onto the “true values” reported by the other instrument (that you hopefully trust).

This lesson is intended to give some ideas on calibration when you have to do it the hard way.

Hard way: Do what you can when you have no true reference to compare against and no real knowledge of the sensor’s actual performance.

This lesson is a semi-serious look at how to handle very cheap sensor products with very poor documentation. If you need actual precision, you need to spend money on a real instrument.

Calibration to the average

If you have many sensors, it’s interesting to have them all measure the same quantity. If you are prepared to assume that their errors have zero mean (an arguable assumption), you can take the average of many measurements as more accurate than a single measurement. The standard deviation of your samples is an estimate of the measurement error.
